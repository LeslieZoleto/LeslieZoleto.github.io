Enchiladas

Ingredients:

  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 2 cups cooked chicken (shredded, can use rotisserie chicken)
  • 2 cups green enchilada sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (Monterey Jack, cheddar, or a Mexican blend)
  • 1/2 cup sour cream (optional, for creaminess)
  • 1 small onion (chopped, optional)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Fresh cilantro (for garnish)
  • Sliced avocado and lime wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven:
    • Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Prepare the Filling:
    • In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat.
    • Add chopped onion and sauté until soft (about 5 minutes).
    • Add shredded chicken and 1/2 cup of green enchilada sauce. Stir to combine and heat through. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Warm the Tortillas:
    • To make the tortillas pliable, wrap them in a damp paper towel and microwave for 30 seconds, or heat them in a dry skillet for a few seconds on each side.
  4. Assemble the Enchiladas:
    • Spread a thin layer of green enchilada sauce in the bottom of a baking dish.
    • Fill each tortilla with 2-3 tablespoons of the chicken mixture, a sprinkle of cheese, and a bit of sour cream (if using).
    • Roll up the tortillas and place them seam-side down in the baking dish.
  5. Top and Bake:
    • Pour the remaining green enchilada sauce over the enchiladas.
    • Sprinkle with the rest of the shredded cheese.
    • Cover with foil and bake for 20 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and slightly golden.
  6. Garnish and Serve:
    • Let the enchiladas rest for a few minutes.
    • Garnish with fresh cilantro, sliced avocado, and serve with lime wedges on the side.

Tips:

  • If you like it extra spicy, add some chopped jalapeños or a few dashes of hot sauce to the chicken filling.
  • Feel free to substitute chicken with cooked ground beef, shredded pork, or black beans for a vegetarian option.
